Canary

Canary is the common name for the small passerine bird Serinus canaria in the finch family, Fringillidae, which has been long been popular as a cage bird. Canaries are native to the Canary Islands, Azores, and Madeira (Clement et al. 1993; Snow and Perrins 1998). In the wild, this bird is sometimes known as the island canary, wild canary, or Atlantic canary.
